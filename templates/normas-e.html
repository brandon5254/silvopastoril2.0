RULES FOR THE PRESENTATION OF PAPERS

The approved works will be published in book format in PDF file that will have its corresponding ISBN.

Deadline for submission of papers: 21/04/2019

they will follow for their organization the structure of scientific works. The maximum length will be six (6) pages, including graphics, tables and images.

Presentation of abstracts (in extenso):

All papers (Poster and Oral presentation) must have:

  • Title
  • Authors
  • Summary
  • Abstract
  • Introduction
  • Materials and Methods
  • Results and Discussion
  • Conclusion
  • Bibliography

In this way, they will appear in the memoirs of Congress.

The working language may be Spanish, English or Portuguese. The document should indicate the number of lines and pages. The pages must be configured in A4 size, with a left margin of 3 cm. and 2.5 cm. in the rest of the margins. Justified text. It is suggested to use Times New Roman font, font size 12, with single spacing. Do not format paragraphs.

The complete body of work must be presented:

The type of presentation: Indicate if the preference is poster presentation or oral presentation.

Title: In bold, using capital letters only in the first letter of the first word; maximum three lines. The title must be in Spanish (or Portuguese) and English. Own nouns that are included must also present the same criteria.

Authors listed after the title: Name initials and full surnames, between authors separated by ; and in italics. Followed by the place of work, postal address and e-mail address of the principal author, or where correspondence will be received; this information should not be in italics.

Abstract in Spanish (or Portuguese) and English (Abstract): It should not exceed 300 words and should contain in a single paragraph Objectives, Materials and Methods, Results and Conclusions.

Keywords in Spanish (or Portuguese) and in English (Keywords): italics (without bold). Five (5) words maximum, not used in the title.

Introduction: It will present the problem, the purpose of the research or project and the reference background.

Materials and Methods: Brief description of the materials, methods, techniques, experimental design and statistical methodology used.

Results and discussion: May go together or separately. The results achieved will be summarized (preliminary or expected in the case of projects). They will be discussed in relation to the question presented in the introduction.

Conclusions: It will synthesize the relevant points of the work and their implications.

Acknowledgements (optional): Arial 9 regular (no bold).

Bibliography: Arial 9 regular (no bold). The list of the bibliography will be ordered alphabetically and should refer to the citations used in the text; in this text the citations will indicate author and year of work, following chronological order, e.g. (Porter et al., 1950; Jones, 1990).

The bibliography will be:

In books

Landsberg, J.J., Gower S.T., 1997. Applications of Physiological Ecology to Forest Management, Academic Press, California, pp. 354.

Chapters of books

Jeffrey, D.W., 1988. Mineral nutrients and the soil environment. En: Jones, M.B., Lazenby, A., (Eds.), The Grass Crop, Chapman and Hall, London, pp. 179-201.

In scientific journals

Lusk, C.H., Contreras, O., Figueroa, J., 1997. Growth, biomass allocation and plant nitrogen concentration in Chilean temperate rainforest tree seedling: effects of nutrient availability. Ecology 109, 49-58.

Proceedings of conferences and congresses

Caldentey, J., Schmidt, H., Bowen, H., 1993. Acumulación de biomasa y nutrientes en rodales de Lenga (Nothofagus pumilio) en Magallanes, Chile. Actas VII Jornadas Técnicas Forestales: Ecosistemas forestales nativos: uso, manejo y conservación. El Dorado, Argentina, pp. 22-36.

Format of tables and graphs: Grayscale Source, Arial 9 regular, for all text included. It is suggested to consider the length of tables and graphs in order not to exceed six pages of maximum length of the work. The legend of the Tables should be placed at the top and the legend of the graphs at the bottom. The graphics should be presented in a separate file in JPG format with a resolution of 300 dpi for best printing.

Image format: JPG format, with 300 dpi resolution. Photos and images must be presented in separate pages.

Papers should be sent to the following e-mail address: xcongreso@silvopastoril2019.org.py

Text files should be sent in *.docx and *.rtf format, so that they maintain writing characteristics, such as bold, superscripts, etc., with the figures and tables inserted in it.

Each file must be identified as follows:

Letter of the subject of the work, as appropriate with the option of the following list, followed by Script, Surname and First name (without space) of the main author. Ex: G-PerezPablo.docx

  • (G) - Sustainable livestock, biodiversity, environment and climate change
  • (P) - Simultaneous production of livestock and forest goods.
  • (S) - Socio-economy in silvopastoral systems.
  • (E) - Productive scenarios in silvopastoral systems (producer testimonies).

For the inclusion of the works in the reports it is necessary that at least one of the authors is duly registered in the Congress.

Modality of Evaluation:

Papers will be evaluated anonymously, i.e. without authors, addresses and quotations from the text that indicate the origin of the paper.

All approved papers will be presented orally or on poster. This information will be communicated to the authors. Once evaluated, papers must be submitted according to the submission guidelines detailed below:

1. GUIDELINES FOR ORAL PRESENTATION

Oral presentations must be composed of the following items:

  • Title
  • Authors
  • Introduction or Background
  • Materials and Methods
  • Results and Discussion
  • Conclusions
  • Acknowledgements (optional)

2. POSTER PRESENTATION GUIDELINES

The posters of the works that are approved for presentation in this modality, will have to be elaborated according to the following guidelines.

Include only:

  • Title (preferably less than 100 characters, excluding spaces).
  • Authors (with institutional and electronic address).
  • Introduction (characterizing the problem and culminating in the objectives).
  • Materials and Methods.
  • Results.
  • Conclusions (if any).

Build sentences (no more than 15 or 20 words), and simple paragraphs, respecting the grammatical and orthographic rules of the language, as well as international symbols and abbreviations.

Prefer pictures, figures and photographs rather than long texts.

Use bold for emphasis, italics for words in another language (Latin, English, etc.), and quotation marks for vulgarisms.

Place simple captions in boxes (top) and figures or photos (bottom).

Use portrait format. Size 110 cm high by 70 cm wide (should be strictly respected), if possible with a margin of 2 cm.

The graphical configuration is convenient that it is on the basis of two or three columns.

Use simple typography not less than 72 points for title, 48 points for sub titles and 30 points for text.

Make sure that the poster can be read in three or four minutes maximum at a distance of 0.9 - 1.50 m.

Avoid that textures or background figures compete visually with texts and other graphic elements.
